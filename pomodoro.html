The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method
by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The technique uses a timer to
break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length,
separated by short breaks. Each interval is known as a "pomodoro",
after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Cirillo used as a university student.
